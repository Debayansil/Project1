Paracetamol

Paracetamol is a widely used over-the-counter medication primarily prescribed to reduce fever and relieve mild-to-moderate pain. It works by blocking pain messengers in the central nervous system, making it a household staple for managing headaches, muscle aches, and cold symptoms..

Amoxicillin

Amoxicillin is a widely prescribed penicillin-type antibiotic used to treat a variety of bacterial infections, such as pneumonia, tonsillitis, and ear or urinary tract infections. It works by stopping the growth of bacteria, making it highly effective against bacterial illnesses but completely ineffective against viral infections like the common cold or flu..

Arnica

Arnica is a popular homeopathic medicine derived from a mountain flower, primarily used topically to reduce swelling, soothe sore muscles, and speed up the healing of bruises. It is widely favored by athletes and individuals seeking a natural remedy for minor aches, sprains, and post-injury recovery..

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

ANSWER: A brand-name drug is the original medicine developed and patented by a company. A generic drug is an identical, lower-cost copy made by other companies after the original patent expires..

TANSWER:Good Manufacturing Practices. These are strict regulatory standards that pharma companies must follow to ensure medicines are consistently produced, safe, and of high quality.

ANSWER: The API is the primary chemical or biological ingredient in a drug that actually produces the intended health effect or cures the disease.

ANSWER: A new drug usually takes 10 to 12 years because it must undergo rigorous multi-phase clinical trials, lab testing, and strict regulatory approvals (like the FDA) to guarantee human safety and effectiveness.

ANSWER:Propaganda Cum Distribution. It is a business model where a pharma company grants marketing and distribution rights for its products to an individual or distributor in a specific geographic area.
